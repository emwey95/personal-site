The Best Movies According to Emily

My favorite movies! Take a look!

La La Land

This is one of my favorite movies for 2 reasons, the soundtrack and the story. The soundtrack is incredibly moving. I got the opportunity to attend a Dallas Symphony Orchestra showing of La La Land, where the orchestra played the soundtrack live to the movie. It was such a powerful and immersive experience.

The story pulls at the heartstrings. It is about 2 artists that are trying their best to make it in their passion. This speaks to me as I was once a musician who also had big dreams. The story also ties in to the romantic side of things, but is not over-the-top or corny. The story as a whole is tragic yet beautiful, as often is life.

A Star is Born

A Star is Born, feauturing Lady Gaga and Bradley Cooper, is a movie that always draws me tears. It takes a deep dive into mental health and the struggles of depression, alcholoism, and the struggles that those carry into relationships. I can relate to this movie on a multitude of levels, and it paints a really great picture of what it is like for someone dealing with major depressing and suicidal thoughts.

The Barbie Movie

This movie does such an incredible job with subtle meanings. Although at first glance, the movie seems to be covered in pink, lighthearted fun, there is a way deeper meaning behind everything going on. Barbie goes on an adventure to figure out who she is in the world. You get taken on a journey to find meaning and purpose, running into obstacles, road blocks, and internal fears and struggles. The first time I saw this in theatres, I had no idea what to expect, but it easily became one of my favorite movies.
